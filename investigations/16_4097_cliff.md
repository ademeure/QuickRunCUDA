# cuBLAS BF16 GEMM Alignment Cliff on B300 (sm_103a, CUDA 13.2)

**TL;DR:** The 4097 cliff is **real and severe** — but the framing is wrong. It is not a "30x slowdown at 4097" vs. 4096. It is a **binary switch in cuBLAS algorithm selection**: power-of-2 (or specific multiples-of-8) sizes get algo 66 (1840+ TFLOPS), every other size falls back to algo 24 (150–300 TFLOPS). The ratio at 4096 vs 4097 is **10.2x**, not 30x.

## Section 2: Is the 4097 cliff real?

**Yes, absolutely.** The 4096→4097 ratio:

- 4096: **1842 TFLOPS**, 0.075 ms, algo 66
- 4097: **180 TFLOPS**, 0.763 ms, algo 24
- **Ratio: 10.2x slower**, not 30x

The original catalog claim of "30x slower" is directionally correct but the magnitude is overstated. The actual ratio is 10x.

## Section 3: Algorithm identification

cuBLAS exposes three distinct algorithms for this problem:

| algo | Behavior | TFLOPS range |
|---|---|---|
| **66** | Fast path — uses tensor cores (tcgen05?) heavily, stagesID=35 | 1500–2250 TFLOPS |
| **23** | Intermediate — partial tile coverage, stagesID=15 | 265–300 TFLOPS |
| **24** | Slow path — fallback for misaligned sizes, stagesID=0 | 150–200 TFLOPS |

The critical finding from Section 4 is that **for M=N=K=4097, cuBLAS only offers algo 24 variants across all 8 returned heuristics.** Algo 66 is never proposed regardless of workspace budget.

## Section 7: Summary and answers

### Is the 4097 cliff real on B300 with CUDA 13.2?
**Yes.** Confirmed. 4096 → 4097 is a 10x performance drop in cuBLAS BF16 GEMM.

### What is the actual TFLOPS gap?
- 4096: **1842 TFLOPS** (90% of the 2251 TFLOPS peak seen at 8192)
- 4097: **180 TFLOPS** (8% of peak — essentially dead compute)
- Ratio: **10.2x**

The original "30x" claim is overstated; 10x is the real number.

### Which sizes show the cliff?
Any M=N=K not in the set {multiples of 8 where cuBLAS's tile fits evenly}. Confirmed fast sizes: 4096, 4104, 4112, 4128, 4160, 8192, 8224, 8256, 16384. Confirmed slow sizes: 4095, 4097–4103, 4105–4111, 4113–4127, 8191, 8193, 16383, 16385.

The fast-path set at the 4K range is approximately: `M ≡ 0 (mod 8)` and `K ≡ 0 (mod 8)` but not all multiples of 8 qualify — specific tile alignment requirements cause further gaps (e.g., 4090, 4092, 4094 are multiples of 2 and get algo 23 at ~280 TFLOPS, but still 6.5x slower than 4096).

### Is it a cuBLAS algorithm selection issue?
**Yes, entirely.** The hardware is the same. cuBLAS selects algo 66 (Blackwell tensor-core path) only for sizes where its tile evenly divides the problem. For 4097, cuBLAS proposes only algo 24 variants across all 8 heuristic slots — the fast algorithm is never considered.

### Can it be mitigated?
**Not through workspace tuning.** The only mitigations are:
1. **Pad M and K to the next multiple of 8 (or ideally 32/64)** — 4097 → 4128 recovers 1755 TFLOPS
2. **Pad to next power of 2** — 4097 → 4096 (trim) or 8192 (next power of 2)
3. **Use a custom kernel** that handles arbitrary sizes — cuBLAS's default heuristic is conservative

The N dimension is less sensitive: N=4097 with M=K=4096 still gets algo 66 at 1778 TFLOPS.
